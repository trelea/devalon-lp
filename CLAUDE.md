# Devalon — Company Landing Page

## Purpose

This site is the public face of **Devalon**, a Software / Tech / AI development & consulting company. Its real job is to **win contract work**: Devalon is how the founder operates as an independent contractor serving multiple clients at once, and this page exists to bring in that work. Every design and copy decision should serve one goal — making a visitor trust Devalon enough to reach out about a project.

## The company

- Devalon provides **development, maintenance, scaling, and custom software / AI solutions** for every kind of customer: **individuals & small clients, startups, and enterprises**.
- The founder has been building software with and for people since school — officially and unofficially — most often helping startups and companies develop their products. The site should carry that personal, been-in-the-trenches credibility: you're hiring a person who has done this their whole career, not a faceless agency.
- Core promise: **we turn ideas into real digital solutions** — real digital manufactures — whether the idea is good or bad, real or delusional. The "even the delusional ideas" wink is intentional brand voice; keep it.

## Voice & tone

- Direct, human, honest. No corporate buzzwords, no jargon.
- Promises are concrete: honest scope, real ballparks, no pressure, no spam.
- Slightly playful, but always credible — the reader should feel they can hand over their idea and it will actually get built.

## What matters when working on this site

- The audience is potential clients of all sizes; nothing should scare off the small individual client or look too small for the enterprise one.
- Contact is deliberately low-friction and personal (direct email, "book a consult") — the site's success is measured in conversations started.
